In my third year at Luca School of Arts, I went on erasmus-trip to Lyon. There I went to ENSBA. In that semester I got the assignment to make a font based on the font of an old newspaper (we could chose between a collection). I choose 'Le Meridional'. This was my first font so it was really exciting. When arriving back in Belgium I continued the assignment, so it was a really interesting trajectory. I choose to present the font in a newspaper with all sorts of newspaper-titles, since the font was based on a old newspaper.

In my second year at Luca School of Arts I got the assignment to make posters on a story that has a book and movie version. I choose 'Fahrenheit 45'. This movie talks about censorship, so I decided to play with that concept in my posters.

In my second year at Luca School of Arts I got the assignment to make a football scarf only using a limited amount of material. The scarf also had to be for a sport of your likings. After some research with the material we could use I made a scarf for the thinking sport 'Go'. I liked the combination of the grid on the board and the little round things you played with. I only used two colors so that it would balance the already busy design on the scraf. By using two colors some elements also are shown better, for example the 'gogogogo' on tha back and the white dots on the front.

This was one of my first assignments in the real world :). Chiro Hekeko always does a little contest for the graphic deisgn of their festival, so I submitted my design and won. Hekeko stands for Heverlee, Kessel-lo and Korbeeklo, so I used the geographical borders on the poster and gave them a 3D effect to let it pop out more. After coming up with this concept I just started playing with the lay-out and came up with this!
